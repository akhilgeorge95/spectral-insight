PHENOLOGY · DETECTION MODEL

A curve for your block. A five-year baseline.

Your block has its own seasonal signature — a shape that repeats. Divergence from that shape is where the interesting information lives.

01 · THE SIGNAL

The reflectance curve changes shape with the season.

In April, a vineyard block's reflectance curve is flat — just bare soil and trellis. By mid-June, as the canopy closes, NIR reflectance climbs and the red-edge sharpens. Through July and August, the curve hits its highest-NIR, deepest-chlorophyll shape. By veraison, subtle shifts show up in the 1450 nm water region. By leaf-fall, the curve collapses back toward soil.

Every block has its own version of this annual shape. Slightly earlier here, slightly later there, slightly taller here, slightly flatter there. After five years, you have a reliable baseline. After that, divergence from the baseline is your alarm.

02 · THE MODEL

A curve fit. A divergence alert.

We fit a parametric curve to each block's seasonal NDVI-plus-water signature. The curve has parameters like 'bud-break start date', 'peak canopy NIR', 'veraison water shift', 'leaf-fall midpoint'. These are the parameters of your block.

Each new season, we fit fresh curves and compute the distance from the five-year rolling average. Small divergences stay within a tolerance band. Large divergences — a block running 8 days ahead, or hitting peak NIR 15% lower than usual — get flagged in the monthly brief with suggested explanations.

This is the detection model that gets more useful every year you're a client. Year one we build the baseline. Year two we start alerting.

04 · A REAL CASE

Veraison, six days early.

In August 2024, a PEC property's two largest blocks showed phenology running 6 days ahead of their five-year baseline. Heat accumulation had been up through July, so that wasn't surprising on its own.

What was surprising: only those two blocks. A neighbouring block on the same property, same variety, was running bang-on baseline. The divergence was real, and local.

The harvest team brought their pick timing forward by four days for those two blocks specifically and left the third on its usual schedule. Titratable acidity at pick came in at target across all three blocks — which, when you're running tight on bottling dates, is exactly the win.

05 · IN YOUR MONTHLY BRIEF

What you'll actually see.

  • Phenology curve with current season overlaid on five-year baseline
  • Per-parameter divergence flags (bud-break, peak canopy, veraison, leaf-fall)
  • Correlation with local GDD (growing degree days) data
  • Suggested harvest-timing adjustments where divergence is large
06 · LIMITS

What year-one clients should know.

  • Baseline-first year.

    Year 1 of data is entry. Year 2 is comparison but noisy. Year 5+ is where the alerts become high-confidence. Be patient.

  • Replants reset the clock.

    If you replant a block mid-history, that block's baseline resets. We track the change explicitly but the divergence signal takes 2–3 years to re-stabilize.

  • Unusually cold springs.

    A cold spring (e.g. 2023) will shift the entire baseline in the same direction across most blocks. The model handles this, but individual-block divergence in such years is hard to interpret.
