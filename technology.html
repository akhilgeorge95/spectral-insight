01 · THE SPECTRUM

Multispectral has a crayon box. Hyperspectral has the rainbow.

A traditional multispectral satellite captures 4–10 broad color buckets — roughly like describing a painting using red, green, blue, and one infrared.

A hyperspectral sensor slices the same light into 200+ razor-thin bands. Each band sees a specific molecular signature — water at 970nm, chlorophyll at 680nm, cell-wall lignin out near 2200nm.

Plant stress doesn't show up as a color shift. It shows up as a shape change in that reflectance curve. Hyperspectral is the only instrument that can see the shape.

02 · THE INSTRUMENT

A push-broom sensor, ten kilometres per second.

The satellites we partner with fly in a sun-synchronous polar orbit at roughly 525 km altitude. As the craft moves, a line of detectors scans the ground beneath it — one row of spectra at a time, tens of thousands of times a second.

Each resulting image is a three-dimensional data cube: length × width × wavelength. A single 1 km × 1 km tile can contain 50+ million individual spectra. Your vineyard is in there somewhere — and so is its signature.

03 · THE MODELS

Trained on Vitis vinifera, not Iowa corn.

Off-the-shelf vegetation indices — NDVI, NDRE, SAVI — were designed for commodity row crops in open-field, hot-summer geometry. They describe a vineyard only loosely.

Our detection models are fine-tuned on three years of paired hyperspectral cubes and ground-truth scout notes from Ontario vineyards specifically. They know what Cabernet Franc leafroll looks like at 680nm in mid-August, in mist, with a north-facing slope.

Every anomaly flag is also reviewed by a human before it leaves our hands. No autopilot reports.

04 · THE PIPELINE

From photons to a paragraph.

PIPE · 01

Atmospheric correction

We strip out the atmosphere — haze, aerosol, water vapor — so what remains is the vine, not the sky between you and the vine.

PIPE · 02

Vine isolation

Row-masking, bare-soil rejection, shadow correction — so you're never measuring the inter-row dust when you think you're measuring canopy.

PIPE · 03

Signature extraction

Per-panel reflectance curves pulled and stored against your own historical baseline. Anomalies are flagged where they diverge.

PIPE · 04

Virus classifier

A lightweight neural net scoring every panel for leafroll / red-blotch / GRBV probability on a 0–1 scale.

PIPE · 05

Water-status model

SWIR band ratios converted to Crop Water Stress Index, per panel, calibrated to your climate zone.

PIPE · 06

Human-in-the-loop

A real agronomist reads the flagged anomalies, writes a short brief, and sends the email. Every time.
