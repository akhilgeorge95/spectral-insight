HYDROLOGY · DETECTION MODEL

Water stress, panel by panel.

Short-wave infrared bands see water the way a well sees wet — directly. Here's how we turn that into an irrigation map.

01 · THE SIGNAL

Water absorbs at 970 nm. The deeper the trough, the wetter the leaf.

Leaf water content is one of the few plant properties that has an almost direct optical signature. Water molecules absorb strongly at specific NIR and SWIR wavelengths — notably 970 nm, 1200 nm, 1450 nm, and 1940 nm. Each shows up in a leaf's reflectance curve as a distinct absorption trough.

When a canopy is under water stress, the troughs fill in. The curve flattens. A healthy Ontario vine in July has a 970 nm trough depth around 0.24. A stressed vine at the same hour might sit at 0.12 — the tissue is physically carrying less water.

BandRoleWhat it reveals
970 nm WATER 1 First water absorption band. Most reliable for mild-stress detection.
1240 nm WATER 2 Secondary band. Confirms 970 nm signal, independent of LAI.
1450 nm WATER 3 Strong trough. Sensitive, but affected by atmosphere.
1650 nm SWIR REF Reference band between water features. Used for normalization.
02 · THE MODEL

CWSI, per panel.

We convert the raw water-absorption signatures into a Crop Water Stress Index (CWSI) per panel. 0.0 is fully hydrated; 1.0 is severe stress. Most healthy Ontario vines sit between 0.08 and 0.22 through the season.

The CWSI is not a raw band ratio — it's calibrated to your climate zone, training system, and prior-year baseline. A CWSI of 0.35 in Niagara in July means something different than the same value in PEC in August.

Paired with your topographic map, the output becomes an irrigation priority list: which blocks, which panels, and in what order.

04 · A REAL CASE

A dripline clog, caught in July.

Mid-July 2024, a Niagara Bench Cabernet Franc block started showing CWSI climbing in rows 15–22 — from 0.14 to 0.38 in two weeks. Everything else on the property read normal.

The grower walked the rows. Canopy looked good. But the dripline on that block segment turned out to have a partial block from root growth and was delivering maybe 40% of its intended flow.

Caught then, it was a thirty-minute fix. Caught at veraison, it would have cost quality points and brix uniformity across the block. The signal was there two weeks before any vine looked 'off'.

06 · LIMITS

Honest about the edges.

  • Recent rainfall.

    The day after a 20 mm rain event, canopy surface water confuses the 970 nm signal for 12–24 hours. We flag these passes in the brief and defer.

  • Heavy morning dew.

    Similar issue. For Ontario mornings we capture windows, our atmospheric correction handles most of it, but extreme dew days will be marked 'deferred'.

  • Rootstock differences.

    Vines on drought-tolerant rootstocks show less 970 nm flattening under the same soil-water deficit. The model is rootstock-aware where you tell us, rootstock-blind where you don't.
