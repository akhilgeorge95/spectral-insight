Leafroll & Red Blotch

A virus that takes a full season to show itself. We catch the chlorophyll signal in week one.

01 · THE SIGNAL

The chlorophyll drop. Before chlorophyll looks different.

Grapevine leafroll-associated viruses (GLRaV-1, GLRaV-3) and grapevine red-blotch virus (GRBV) both suppress photosynthetic activity long before the canopy visibly reddens or yellows. That suppression shows up at 680 nm — the chlorophyll absorption band — as a measurable flattening of the reflectance trough.

The red-edge region (690–760 nm) also shifts its inflection point. A healthy Cabernet Franc has its red-edge inflection at roughly 721 nm. A GLRaV-3 infected vine of the same variety, asymptomatic to the eye, sits at 716 nm or below. Five nanometres. Detectable. Invisible to you.

BandRoleWhat it reveals
680 nm CHLOROPHYLL Direct absorption band. Depth of trough indicates chlorophyll a/b content.
720 nm RED EDGE Inflection point. Shifts blue-ward under virus stress.
750 nm NIR PLATEAU Reference band. Canopy structure, independent of pigment.
850 nm NIR Cellular scattering. Baseline for normalization.
02 · THE MODEL

A classifier trained on three years of Ontario vines.

Our virology model is a convolutional classifier fine-tuned on a training set of paired hyperspectral cubes and grower-confirmed virus status — 430 blocks across Niagara Peninsula, Prince Edward County, and Lake Erie North Shore, spanning 2023–2025.

It outputs a per-panel probability score between 0 and 1. Panels above 0.6 enter the monthly brief as a watchlist; panels above 0.85 get a recommended ground-confirmation walk.

We deliberately do not train across regions. A Californian leafroll signature looks slightly different from an Ontario one — we picked depth over breadth.

04 · A REAL CASE

Twelve vines, three weeks early.

In late June 2024 a 14-hectare Beamsville Bench property showed a cluster of twelve panels in block 2 with virus probabilities climbing from 0.3 to 0.7 across two consecutive passes. The grower walked the rows, couldn't see a thing, and called us to confirm.

We sampled. ELISA came back GLRaV-3 positive on eight of the twelve. By the time visible leaf-roll symptoms appeared in late August, the affected vines had already been marked, the neighbouring rows had been inspected, and the removal plan was written. The outbreak stayed under 30 vines.

Without the early flag, the usual story: spread, confusion about the cause, a whole block under suspicion, and a two-season cleanup.

06 · LIMITS

What we can't tell you (yet).

  • Variety-specific calibration.

    The model currently treats Cabernet Franc, Merlot, and Pinot Noir with the same baseline. Variety-specific tuning is on the 2026 roadmap.

  • Distinguishing virus from stress.

    Severe water stress and advanced leafroll can look similar at 680 nm. We cross-check with the water-stress model before flagging, but in extreme drought the signal is genuinely ambiguous.

  • Early spring.

    Before full canopy close-up (typically pre-bloom), pixel mixing with soil makes virus scoring unreliable. We start scoring at around E-L stage 17.
