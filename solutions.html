Each of our detection models is a dedicated pipeline — built, tuned, and validated on Ontario vineyards. Here's what every one of them actually does.

01 · VIROLOGY

Leafroll & Red Blotch detection

Grapevine leafroll-associated viruses and red-blotch-associated virus both depress photosynthetic efficiency long before visual symptoms show. The reflectance shape shifts in the red edge (690–760 nm) and near-infrared plateau.

Our classifier scores every canopy pixel against a panel-level virus probability and flags suspect zones as early as mid-June — often a full growing season before a scout would note change.

02 · HYDROLOGY

Water stress mapping

Short-wave infrared bands around 970 nm and 1450 nm are direct absorbers of leaf water content. Stressed tissue shows a distinctive flattening across those troughs — even when the canopy still looks green.

We convert that signal into a per-panel Crop Water Stress Index so you can irrigate where it actually matters, not on a blanket schedule, and avoid over-watering your reds.

03 · NUTRITION

Nutrient deficiency mapping

Nitrogen deficiency manifests as a blue-shift in the chlorophyll absorption peak. Potassium and magnesium show up further out in the red-edge. Each has its own fingerprint.

The model identifies anomaly clusters and suggests which tissue-test sites would confirm the diagnosis — turning your petiole sampling into informed sampling, not guesswork.

04 · CANOPY

Vigor & uniformity zones

Canopy density is the classical use-case for satellite imagery — but hyperspectral adds decomposition. We can tell you whether a low-vigor zone is low because of water, virus, nutrition, or simply because the soil changes.

The output is a four-zone management map that matches what your tractor driver already intuits — now made explicit, and differentiated by underlying cause.

05 · PHENOLOGY

Season tracking & baseline

Reflectance curves change shape over the season — flat in April, peaking at mid-summer, shifting again at veraison. Your block has its own curve. Our model builds a five-year baseline of it.

When the current season diverges from the baseline, the report tells you where and by how much. Advance or delay, zone by zone.

Frost & heat-risk exposure

Using your own topography, aspect, and elevation — derived from the same satellite stack — we rank each block's vulnerability to late-spring frost and mid-summer heat-spike events.

Paired with Ontario climate history, this becomes a forward-looking exposure score: which blocks to protect first, which blocks buffer themselves.
