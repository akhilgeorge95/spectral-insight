NUTRITION · DETECTION MODEL

Nitrogen, potassium, magnesium — resolved.

Each nutrient leaves a distinct spectral fingerprint. Instead of blanket spraying, spray the panels that actually need it.

01 · THE SIGNAL

Pigment chemistry has wavelength-specific signatures.

Nutrient deficiency manifests through pigment changes. Nitrogen deficiency reduces overall chlorophyll (drops the 550 nm green reflectance peak). Potassium deficiency shifts the red-edge inflection. Magnesium deficiency affects the 680 nm absorption depth in a specific, sub-band pattern.

A hyperspectral cube contains all of these simultaneously. The challenge isn't seeing the signal — it's disentangling which deficiency is causing which shift, since some of them overlap. That's what our element-specific regressors are built for.

BandRoleWhat it reveals
550 nm GREEN PEAK Chlorophyll a/b total concentration. Drops under N deficit.
680 nm CHLOROPHYLL Mg-specific sensitivity in trough shape.
720 nm RED EDGE Inflection point shifts under K deficit.
1510 nm PROTEIN N-bearing compound overtone. Rare but diagnostic.
02 · THE MODEL

Three models, one per element.

We train a separate regressor for each of N, K, and Mg. Each is calibrated against petiole-test ground truth from 90+ Ontario blocks. Individual-element modeling is slightly less elegant than a unified classifier, but it produces cleaner per-nutrient anomaly maps.

The output is not a hard diagnosis — it's a ranked list of panels where a tissue test would most likely confirm an actionable deficiency. We pair this with your historical tissue data where you have it.

The point is to turn petiole sampling from a blanket 10-random-panels exercise into informed, targeted sampling.

04 · A REAL CASE

A spray campaign that was not needed.

A 24-hectare PEC property was preparing a property-wide magnesium foliar spray in late July 2025 based on a handful of symptomatic vines in one corner. We had just delivered their July brief.

Our Mg deficiency map flagged 18 panels across two adjacent rows — all in that same corner, none elsewhere. The rest of the property read normal.

They switched to a targeted treatment of those 18 panels. Saved the cost and labour of 98% of the spray. The localized deficiency turned out to be a historical soil-amendment gap from a replant three years earlier.

06 · LIMITS

Where the signal gets noisy.

  • Iron and micronutrients.

    We don't currently score Fe, Zn, B, or Mn. Their spectral signatures are weaker and less studied for grapes specifically. 2026 road-map.

  • Variety sensitivity.

    White varieties show weaker chlorophyll anomalies in general. Detection is slightly less sensitive on Riesling and Chardonnay blocks vs. reds.

  • Seasonal timing.

    Early-season deficiency (pre-bloom) is hard to score. We recommend nutrient-focused reports from June onward.
